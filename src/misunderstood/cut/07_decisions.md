#### Freya

Freya almost dozed off with her head in Merlin's lap. Merlin fell silent, as he absent mindedly rubbed her head with the tips of his fingers, with the pressure just the way she liked it. Freya could almost purr. 

When Merlin's hands stopped scratching her head, she sat up, noticing his purturbed expression. 

*What is it?* Freya telepathically asked Merlin. Freya did hear Leon say something about other servants being attacked by Arthur. This was not new information, but she wanted to let Merlin decide exactly how much to reveal to Leon.

Merlin then waved his hand. George and Leon froze where they stood. Leon had inhaled, and not yet exhaled. George held his quill above the ink pot, and a drop of ink stayed frozen in mid air. 

A slightly immaterial double of Merlin's body, grabbed her hand, pulling her up from her seated position, leaving her body behind, as he helped her step into a doorway.  Freya loved that she never quite got used to Merlin bringing her into the Ephemeral Plane. It was always a neat way to be strategic. Time was paused, so they could do what they needed to do. Nobody else experienced any time passing.

&ldquo;How much do you trust Leon?&rdquo; Freya asked, sitting down on the not-quite-solid carpet, in the room Merlin made. This particular physical manefestation of the Ephemeral Plane looked just like their own noncorporal living room, except the wall and door that typically opened into their bedroom, was left open, to allow them to see their own bodies, and a frozen version of Arthur, back turned to Leon's door at the moment. His body was currently blocking the body of a young girl. She was likely in distress, and Freya wished there was more she could do, without risking Merlin's execution.

She turned back to Merlin, happy that he did this intentionally.  Here, in his plane, he stood tall, like the powerful sorcer. Freya, shifted to a kneeling position, while she nuzzled right between Merlin's legs.

#### Gwenivere

At long last, the door to the armory door opened.  The tall, blonde man, styled as a king walked in. Gwen gripped one of her daggers more tightly than was strictly necessary, in order to steady herself.  She hoped that she did not betray any weaknesses. 

"What have you got for me today?" Arthur asked, sauntering towards her. She was happy that three tables and at least two distinct weapons stands stood between her and the king. 

"Place your requests on the table, your *Majesty*," Gwen said, indicating at one of the tables.  Arthur wanted some weapons for his most special knights. Luckily, these *happen* to be *exactly* the knights that Merlin should most watch out for. 

*Take a deep breath. Don't betray the degree of your nerves,* Gwen thought to herself.  She straightened her shoulders, hoping to hide her superior chainmail from view.

"I said, what do you have for *me*," Arthur said, licking his lips. The armory was not well ventallated.  A pool of sweat dripped from Arthur's upper lip.  He might have been handsome if he weren't so cruel.  

Gwen simultaneously drew her ordinary sword, and her dagger, moving the sword forward, angling some etching for Arthur. 

"I can etch each knight's name on the blade like so," she said, in a measured tone, displaying the etching of her own name on the flat of the blade.  She had considered writing Lancelot's name, but decided against revealing more than she needed of herself. 

Arthur swatted the blade away, with his gloved hand, "Youch!"

Gwen tried not to giggle, "It is sharp. Did you not know that this is a *sword*"

"How dare you raise your weapon at royalty?" Arthur raised one eyebrow.  

"I was *trying* to show the sword you requested," Gwen responded.

She did not use any honorifics.

#### Merlin

From underneath the bed, Merlin could hear Leon agreeing with Arthur&rsquo;s assumption that Freya was an *interchangable* whore.  *Good* he thought.  This meant that he was going along with whatever assumptions Arthur made.  

When Leon had shooed Arthur off, with whatever official paperwork that was demanded of him, Merlin dared to exhale, relaxing enough to let go of the bedframe. 

He almost bumpted his head as he simmied out from under the bed, and relaxed as Freya started giggling at his weirdly quaffed hair, no doubt from the underside of the mattress.

&ldquo;Leon,&rdquo; Merlin started, swallowing a giggle, and sigh of relief, &ldquo;Freya and I have some explaining to do.&rdquo;

As we walked back over to join Freya on the bed, Merlin glanced at George, trying to decipher how much of this was going to be a surprise.  

&ldquo;First, thank you for keeping my presence hidden from Arthur.&rdquo; Merlin continued, &ldquo;I would ultimately be fine, but well, I now feel safe telling you this: *Forbyrn*&rdquo; Merlin stood up, walking a bit closer to Leon, still anxious about revealing the small flame in his hand.

George had a grin the width of the White Mountains. *So George knew already,* Merlin telepathically told Freya.

Leon just sat in his chair, eyes unblinking, focused on the flame. 

&ldquo;Both of us&hellip; That is how we are, well, this is why we don't have a plethora of royal basterds puttering around the place, the way Arthur fucks.&rdquo; 

Merlin receieved a plethora of images of Gwen scolding Lancelot about dishes, from Freya. Unsure of what to say next, he sent Freya an memory of herself with soap bubbles on her nose. 

&ldquo;What Merlin is trying to say, &rdquo; Freya started, helpfully, &ldquo;Is that we sometimes use magic to help vicitms of the King, if requested&rdquo;

George, after taking a deep breath said, &ldquo; So *that* is how you managed to sharpen a dozen swords in an afternoon, and still have time to tailor his magesties undergarments before breakfast&rdquo;

Merlin, chuckled at that, momentarily forgetting that he could not just telepathically admonish George with images of his own refelected upside down in a helmet. 

&ldquo;I assume you meant for this to be a secret?&rdquo; George asked, in a slightly more serious tone.

&ldquo;No. I meant to bradcast it so that I could become a target, and put Camelot into *even* more danger,&rdquo; Merlin responded sarcastically. 

This earned him a smack on the leg from Freya. 
&ldquo;This *is* shocking to some people,&rdquo; she teased, pushing her hair back, and leaning onto his shoulder, and looping her arm through his.

This innocent gesture seemed to startle Leon back into the conversation.

&ldquo;Lets take moment. Freya, are *you* okay?&rdquo; He paused for a moment, making looking directly at Freya.  Merlin shifted his arm to be protectively around her. When Freya did not answer, he continued, &ldquo;You do not need to take such treatment from Merlin, any more than Merlin does from Arthur.  Are you aware that it is within your right to refuse a man&rsquo;s advances. &rdquo; 

Leon then looked into Merlin&rsquo;s eyes with an intensity that could burn down a bridge, &ldquo;Unhand her please, and allow her to speak,&rdquo; he commanded. 

Merlin took a deep breath, but moved his arm to be near Freya, allowing her to grab onto him when she wanted to. He stared back at Leon, resolute. The scratching of Geroge&rsquo;s quill sounded as loud as the bugels that anounced the king&rsquo;s arrival.

It was at this moment that George knocked his inkwell over, clattering to the ground, cutting the tension. The ink went everywhere, staining Freya&rsquo;s shoes, and seeping into the fringes of the carpet behind them. 

George was doubled over in a fit of silent giggles. It was almost unnerving seeing the awkward creases of George&rsquo;s perfectly pressed uniform, and the little flecks of ink spilled onto his otherwise pristinely white shirt.

&ldquo;Haa&mdash;Leon&mdash;Hehehehe&rdquo; George managed to wheeze between giggles, &ldquo;Those two are very married. And part of Merlin *has magic*. Do you think they did not *talk* about the little show they put on?&rdquo;

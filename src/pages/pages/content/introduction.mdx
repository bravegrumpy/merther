## {frontmatter.title}

This an aggregation of fan works based on the BBC Show Merlin.

Most of the fan works listed have adult content. The is separated into the explicitness rating categories, as seen on [AO3](https://archiveofourown.org/faq/posting-and-editing?language_id=en#posteditratings). Each rating group will then be split into works I wrote, and other people's works, that I like and want to feature.

If a section does not exist, then I don't have anything to put there yet.

***

The works that I wrote myself are hosted here and also published on AO3. The works that I'm recommending, by other people, simply link to the AO3 posting.

If you see a link to your work and do not want it linked here, [please email me](mailto:writing@bravegrumpy.com), and I will happily remove all references to your work.

I am pairing the [AO3 ratings](https://archiveofourown.org/faq/posting-and-editing?language_id=en#posteditratings) with [cute visuals](https://www.mabsland.com/Adoption.html) of &ldquo;TV&rdquo; ratings.

This site is built to create a visually interesting reading experience, on an old-web kind of aesthetic.  Clearly, I have used some modern CSS features, such as [:clip-path](https://www.w3.org/TR/css-masking/#clipping) [](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference/Properties/clip-path) or [:masonry grid](https://www.w3.org/TR/css-grid-3/#background).  However, I captured the vibes from websites of yesteryear, using modern tooling.  I wrote all the styling myself, using [:LESS]( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Less_(style_sheet_language) ) [](https://lesscss.org/about/) as a preprocessor.  This allowed me to make my CSS code a little more [:DRY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don%27t_repeat_yourself), while still sticking fairly close to the raw CSS. One of my future goals with this project is to figure out how to turn this mess of a project into an 
[Astro theme](https://astro.build/themes/). 

Beyond the CSS styling, there are also a few visual assets, such as the embroidery, and the paper texture. I purchased these from an [asset library](https://www.textures.com/).  They are *not* AI generated. There are a grand total of 11 images within this site that were AI generated.  They are marked, and isolated to [:a single page](/other/images#aiTitle).  I do not plan on adding any more AI nor do I endorse the use of generative AI.
